Create a Q2 (April-June 2026) social media content plan for Talha & Aqdas Co.

We are a small business (5 people). Our LinkedIn presence is minimal right now.

# Q2 2026 LinkedIn Content Plan

## Recommended Posting Frequency

**2-3 posts per week** on LinkedIn.
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Best times: 8-10 AM or 12-1 PM (your local timezone)
- Mix of post types: text insights, short stories, questions to audience

---

## April 2026 — Theme: "Who We Are"
*Goal: Introduce the company and build familiarity with new followers*

### Post 1 — Founder Story
**Format:** Personal text post (with photo of Talha)
> "5 years ago I started Talha & Aqdas Co. with one goal in mind: [your mission here].
> Today we are a team of 5 and still driven by that same mission.
> Here is what we have learned along the way..."
*Why it works: Personal founder stories consistently get 3-5x more engagement than company announcements.*

### Post 2 — Meet the Team
**Format:** Carousel or photo post
> "The people behind Talha & Aqdas Co. Small team. Big impact."
> Feature each team member with their role and one fun fact.
*Why it works: Humanises the brand and builds trust with potential clients.*

### Post 3 — What We Do (and Who We Do It For)
**Format:** Short text post
> "We help [target client type] to [specific outcome].
> If you are struggling with [pain point], let us talk."
*Why it works: Clear positioning attracts the right clients and generates inbound enquiries.*

---

## May 2026 — Theme: "What We Know"
*Goal: Establish expertise and thought leadership*

### Post 4 — Industry Insight
**Format:** List post (5-7 bullet points)
> "5 things most small businesses get wrong about [your industry topic]:
> 1. ...
> 2. ..."
*Why it works: Educational content gets saved and shared — extending your reach beyond followers.*

### Post 5 — Behind the Scenes
**Format:** Short story or photo
> "Here is what a typical Tuesday looks like at our office..."
> Show your process, tools, or team in action.
*Why it works: Transparency builds credibility and makes the brand more relatable.*

### Post 6 — Client Win (anonymised)
**Format:** Short case study text
> "A client came to us with [problem]. 6 weeks later: [result].
> Here is exactly what we did..."
*Why it works: Social proof is the most persuasive content type for B2B audiences.*

---

## June 2026 — Theme: "Looking Ahead"
*Goal: Generate conversations and attract new clients for Q3*

### Post 7 — Mid-Year Reflection
**Format:** Personal text post
> "We are halfway through 2026. Here is what surprised us, what worked, and what we are doubling down on in Q3."
*Why it works: Reflective posts position you as self-aware and growth-oriented.*

### Post 8 — Question to Your Audience
**Format:** Engagement post
> "Quick question for fellow business owners: what is the ONE thing you wish you had sorted earlier in your business journey? Drop it in the comments."
*Why it works: Questions dramatically boost comments, which signals quality content to the LinkedIn algorithm.*

### Post 9 — Q3 Preview / Offer
**Format:** Announcement post
> "Exciting things coming at Talha & Aqdas Co. this July. If you have been thinking about working with us, now is a great time to reach out."
*Why it works: Creates urgency and gives warm followers a clear next step.*
